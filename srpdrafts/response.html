OpenLCB CAN Physical Spec and its Relation to NMRA S9.5.1

Background

The NMRA is trying to develop a model railroad local control bus to carry MRR-accessory information. Several pre-existing projects have been in process that were put forward as candidates for the eventual NMRAnet(R). These include: S9.5, led by Don Voss, OpenLCB (designated S9.6 by the NMRA), MERG CBUS led by Mike Bolton and Gil Fuchs, and AtBus, led by Roger Edwards.

Development of a Proposed Standard and its associated Technical Note

Members of this community (BJ, AS, JD, and DH) joined an ad hoc NMRA committee to develop a common CAN physical specification. This committee was formed at the Milwaukee Convention, and included membership from the the above mentioned projects, and two manufacturers, Silicon Railway and RRCirKits. The committee was under the auspices of Di Voss, and Karl Kobel was tasked as chair. The aforementioned OpenLCB members wrote the CAN Physical Spec, S9.x.1 (here) and associated Technical Note,TN9.x.1 (here) for consideration of the NMRA Board as a NMRA Standard.

Don Voss, as S9.5 representative, was part of the committee, and put forward extensions to the International CAN Spec ISO 11898-1; to require an increase (20k) to the minimum Rdiff parameter of the drivers; and to require a split-termination of the bus. These items were duly discussed and the committee felt that modifying an international standard would lead to suspect advantages and definite disadvantages. The advantages claimed were increased number of nodes and bus length, which were based on limited theoretical information in two manufacturers datasheets. The disadvantages included:

  • It will exclude commercially available equipment that is ISO 11898-1 compliant;

  • It will likely lead to NMRAnet compliant systems that will fail. This will be very difficult for the end user to both detect and correct without sophisticated equipment.

These suggestions were eventually not required and likely to make the resulting standard unstable and was rejected. Don Voss at this point left the group.

The committee duly submitted the S9.x.1 and TN9.x.1 documents via Karl Kobel to the NMRA Standards Manager Didrik Voss, who is the brother of Don Voss.

Actions of the NMRA Board and Its Agents

Subsequent to their submission, without communication to or consultation with the committee, Didrik Voss modified the documents, including the rejected Don Voss suggestions, and deleting the power distribution across the bus. He then publiched both sets of documents onto the NMRA website, again without notification of the committee or authors. Despite extensive criticisms the Don Voss changes were retained, although he did retract the power-distribution before his final submission to the board.

In a closed meeting in February, the NMRA Board accepted the changed document. As of the date of the writing, the committee has not been informed of the decision, nor the reasoning behind it. We are aware that the modified S9.x.1 has been published as S9.5.1 on the NMRA webite, with the authors names deleted. We note that this numbering suggests that the is is part of the S9.5 proposals, ie part of Don Voss's proposed S9.5.

We have serious difficulties with the above process:

  • The original S/TN9.x.1 documents are copyrighted material. The NMRA has published derivative works without seeking nor obtaining permission. We reserve our right to seek legal remedies.

  • Numbering the posted standard as S9.5.1 makes a farce of the NMRAnet process. The ongoing process has lead to the loss of talent from the NMRA's available pool of expertise, and casts ridicule on the NMRA.

  • It displays the blatant bias that Didrik Voss, and the Board as proxy, towards his brother Don, contrary to the Bylaws of the NMRA. A formal review of these Bylaw violations will be sought from the NMRA President and Board.
