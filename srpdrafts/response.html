OpenLCB statement on recent NMRA actions

OpenLCB CAN Physical Spec and its Relation to NMRA S9.5.1

Background: The NMRA is trying to develop a model railroad local control bus to carry MRR-accessory information. Several pre-existing projects have been in process that were put forward as candidates for the eventual NMRAnet(R). These include: S9.5, led by Don Voss, OpenLCB (designated S9.6 by the NMRA), MERG CBUS led by Mike Bolton and Gil Fuchs, and AtBus, led by Roger Edwards.

Members of this community (BJ, AS, JD, and DH) joined an ad hoc NMRA committee to develop a common CAN physical specification. This committee was formed at the Milwaukee Convention, and included membership from the the above mentioned projects, and two manufacturers, Silicon Railway and RRCirKits. The committee was under the auspices of Di Voss, and Karl Kobel was tasked as chair. The aforementioned OpenLCB members wrote the CAN Physical Spec (here) and associated Technical Note (here) for consideration of the NMRA Board as a NMRA Standard.

Don Voss, as S9.5 representative, was part of the committee, and put forward extensions to the International CAN Spec ISO 11898-1; to require an increase (20k) to the minimum Rdiff parameter of the drivers; and to require a split-termination of the bus. These items were duly discussed and the committee felt that modifying an international standard would lead to suspect advantages and definite disadvantages. The advantages claimed were increased number of nodes and bus length, which were based on limited theoretical information in two manufacturers datasheets. The disadvantages included:

  • It will exclude commercially available equipment that is ISO 11898-1 compliant;

  • It will likely lead to NMRAnet compliant systems that will fail. This will be very difficult for the end user to both detect and correct without sophisticated equipment.

These suggestions were eventually not required and likely to make the resulting standard unstable and was rejected. Don Voss at this point left the group.

The committee duly submitted the S9.x.1 and TN9.x.1 documents via Karl Kobel to the NMRA Standards Manager Didrik Voss, who is the brother of Don Voss. Didrik Voss modified the documents, without communication or consultation with the committee, as per Don Voss's suggestions. He also deleted power distribution across the bus aspect, although he retracted this before his final submission.

In a *closed* meeting in February, the NMRA Board accepted the changed document. None of this was communicated to teh committee members before the document was posted as S9.5.1, which is the designation for the Don Voss proposal.

We have serious difficulties with the above process:

  • The original S/TN9.x.1 documents are copyrighted material. The NMRA has published derivative works without seeking nor obtaining permission. We reserve our right to seek legal remedies.

  • Numbering the posted standard as S9.5.1 makes a farce of the NMRAnet process. It has lead to the loss of talent from the NMRA's available pool of expertise, and casts ridicule on the NMRA.

  • It displays the blatant bias that Didrik Voss, and the Board as proxy, towards his brother Don, contrary to the Bylaws of the NMRA. A formal review of these Bylaw violations will be sought from the NMRA President and Board.
