OpenLCB Specifications and Explanations

This page provides access to the OpenLCB Specifications and Explanations documents.

OpenLCB is not a formal standards-making body, but we are interested in making it clear what's required and not required for interoperation between OpenLCB nodes. Therefore, we are creating a series of "specifications" and corresponding "explanations" as specific documents. “Specifications” are normative, to the extent that anything this bunch of happy campers does can be proscriptive. “Explanations” are just that, coupled statements of explanations and additional information that go with each Specification. For more information on the conventions used when writing OpenLCB specifications and explanations, please see the separate “conventions” page.

For background information, informal and development documents, etc, please see the “documentation” index page.

Layering

OpenLCB has been created as a layered family of protocols. The basic structure is along the lines of the wildly successful architecture of the ARPA TCP/IP family of protocols. For use on e.g. Controller Area Network (CAN), OpenLCB has to deal with lower-level concepts than the TCP/IP stack does, however, so OpenLCB is structured like a hybrid of the TCP/IP and ISO models.

Allocation Tables

Various numbers have to be allocated and recorded in OpenLCB. This section documents those.

Numbers can have one of several status values:

  • Adopted - in use, and will not change

  • Proposed - in use, but not yet adopted, and may change

  • Reserved - allocated to a future purpose, but not yet in use

  • Available - not yet allocated
