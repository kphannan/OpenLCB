OpenLCB Specifications and Explanations

OpenLCB is not a formal standards-making body, but we are interested in making it clear what's required and not required for interoperation between OpenLCB nodes. Therefore, we are creating a series of "specifications" and corresponding "explanations" as specific documents. “Specifications” are normative, to the extent that anything this bunch of happy campers does can be proscriptive. “Explanations” are just that, coupled statements of explanations and additional information that go with each Specification.

Layering

OpenLCB has been created as a layered family of protocols. The basic structure is along the lines of the wildly successful architecture of the ARPA TCP/IP family of protocols. For use on e.g. Controller Area Network (CAN), OpenLCB has to deal with lower-level concepts than the TCP/IP stack does, however, so OpenLCB is structured like a hybrid of the TCP/IP and ISO models.
